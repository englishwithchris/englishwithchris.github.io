Terms and conditions of service

Applicability: These terms and conditions of service apply to all lessons and interactions undertaken with the teacher and no exceptions may be made unless with the direct written consent of the teacher. The spirit of these rules aims to provide a safe, welcoming, and fair learning environment for both the teacher and the students. In the event that a situation arises that is not covered by these terms, a decision will be made by the teacher in accordance with the spirit of the rules. All students who undertake lessons with the teachers agree to be bound by these rules.

Availability of service: The teacher provides online English classes via video-chat software within the business hours detailed on this website. Teaching services are provided at the discretion of the teacher. The teacher considers applications from any student aged 18 or over. The final decision on the provision of service is at the discretion of the teacher. Normally, all students are accepted for teaching, however, if the teacher feels that they cannot successfully teach the student for whatever reason, then this decision is final.

Equal opportunities policy: All students, regardless of educational background, nationality, race, gender, religion, disability, or other legally protected characteristics are welcome to apply to become a student with the teacher. The teacher is committed to providing a fair, honest, and open service for all, regardless of background.

Payments: Payment for packages or individual lessons must be made in advance of the first paid lesson, and are payable by PayPal or bank transfer. All associated fees (bank transfer, PayPal expenses, card charges, currency exchange fees, and others are the responsibility of the student.

If payment is not received in advance of the first class, the class will be canceled with no liability to the teacher.

Missed lessons: In the event that the student misses a lesson for whatever reason, the teacher reserves the right to consider this lesson completed and charge the student the full value of the lesson.  In the event that the teacher misses the lesson for whatever reason, the student will not be charged. Each case will be assessed on a case-by-case basis, and the teacher will endeavor to find a fair solution each time.

Late attendance: In the event of student non–attendance the teacher will remain available until 15 minutes after the scheduled lesson start time. After this time has elapsed the teacher will no longer be available and the lesson will be considered missed, with the full cost of the class payable to the teacher.

Student unable to attend: If the student is unable to attend the lesson as scheduled for whatever reason, they must provide at least 24-hours notice of this fact and the lesson may be rescheduled without charge. If less than 24-hours notice is given, the teacher reserves the right to charge full value for the lesson and consider the lesson completed, but will assess each occurrence on a case-by-case basis and will endeavor to find a fair solution each time. The decision of the teacher in this matter will be final.

Technical problems: In the event that a technological problem causes the lesson to be abandoned, if the lesson has not begun or less than 15 minutes has elapsed the lesson will be allowed for rescheduling at no charge. 

Refer a friend promotion: For each friend, colleague, or associate that the student refers to the teacher, the student will be eligible to receive one free one-hour lesson. Referrals must have booked at least one paid class for the referring student to be eligible.

Free trial class: The teacher offers a 30-minute trial class free-of-charge to new students. No more than one free trial class may be booked per student. Only qualified students may book a free trial class.

Free trial class: qualified students: to qualify for a free trial class, the student must:

  • Not be an existing student of the teacher
  • Not have previously attended a free trial class with the teacher
  • Not be below the age of 18
  • Communicate with the teacher before the class, at least including the following information:
    • Name
    • Current English level
    • Objectives for taking classes

Whilst most students are accepted for a free trial class, the teacher reserves the right to refuse service. This may happen if:

  • The student does not meet the above criteria
  • The teacher feels that they cannot effectively teach the student (in which case a reasoned refusal will be provided)
  • Incomplete, inaccurate, or dishonest information is provided by the student.
  • The teacher feels that the application for a free class is not made in good faith or believes that the application constitutes an abuse or a potential abuse of the service.
  • The decision of the teacher is final in all cases.

Refunds: In the event that the student wishes to cancel a package for whatever reason, the value of any unused lessons will be returned to the student in full. Any lessons that have been completed will not be available for refund.

Student code of conduct: The teacher is committed to treating all students with respect. Students are expected to treat the teacher with respect and to behave in a way that does not interfere with or cause disruption to the normal operations of the class. Racist, xenophobic, sexual, aggressive, or discriminatory language or behavior of any kind on the part of the student will be grounds for immediate class abandonment, cancellation of future classes, and refusal of future lesson requests.

Complaints: The teacher endeavors to provide a high-quality, fair service for all. In the unlikely event that a student wishes to make a complaint, this complaint will be treated with respect and fairness by the teacher. 

Cookies: This website does not use cookies for any purpose. However, third-party services may use cookies, in which case the student is subject to the cookie policy of those services.
